Originally the plan was to shoot in Osaka and then spend a week traveling around Japan. After failing to find someone to host our Osaka show, we ending up doing an episode in Tokyo and spending our first week in Japan traveling. It has been a life goal of mine to make it to Japan, and it was well worth the wait. We started our stay in Osaka. On first sight Osaka is a large, layered, clean, and organized city. We stayed in Umeda, which is the business / train / hotel / department store hub.

After 2 fun days of working in the hotel we finally made it out for a day in the city. Dinner was amazing yakitori.

It was the perfect way to start a Do¯tombori pub crawl. Most buildings were filled with bars on every floor that created for endless options.

Eventually we found ourselves in an Irish Pub that was celebrating its 18th anniversary. It was Guinness, bad music, drunk expats, and a Canadian gal that wanted to introduce me to an authentic Japanese experience. The idea of a ‘love hotel’ brings to mind plastic sheets, heart shaped beds, and disturbing smells. Not so in Japan. This was nicer than most places we stayed at on this trip.

The “menu” of rooms to choose from. All with different themes depending on your flavor.

After 3 days in Osaka and 7 straight weeks in cities, it was time to head out the the countryside. Our first stop was Mt. Koya San to cleanse ourselves and pray with the Buddhist monks.

The town map.

Morning prayer was followed by a walk through the beautiful Okunoin Cemetery.

The next stop was Kinosaki, an Onsen town north of Osaka. It was 2 days of kimonos, wooden sandals, hot springs, ice cream, and crab feasts.

Love hotels, monks, and Onsens. Everything one needs to prepare for a week in Tokyo.
