In big news, we’ve switched our photo format to 16:9. Prepare to experience this blog in widescreen. It’s going to rock your world. Where was I? Ah Beirut. Yes. Beirut.

In every city we’ve gone to so far we’ve ended up in a flea market. Which leads me to conclude that there must be flea markets in every city. Shocking. Beirut’s Souk Al-Ahad market was pretty full on though. We hear Syrian or Egyptian markets are even crazier if you’re into flea market extreme sports. But the clothes car was cool.

And stereo guy.

And the animal section. Birds, fish, turtles, chickens, rabbits, cats – a literal flea market. Ha.

Our host Pedros was cool and collected. As were we. Especially whilst being photographed by the talented Tanya Traboulsi.

Photo by Tanya Traboulsi

It rained torrential style for five days straight in Beirut and according to Pedros it hadn’t rained in the seven years before our arrival... The Lebanon? It’s really nice there. Pity about the Irish weather.

The rain didn’t stop us though. We got our feet wet and met a lot of interesting artists, musicians and general troublemakers. Katibe 5, Scrambled Eggs and The Incompetents were some of the bands we hooked up with. All are sharp by the way. Have a listen. This is from the Scrambled Eggs gig on Halloween night.

On Sunday we went to the sea and managed to spend an hour outside in between showers. Turkish coffee, rolled cigarettes and new friends. Shades of blue and grey. Widescreen memories of Beirut.
