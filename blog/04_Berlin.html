Berlin is beautiful. We’ve arrived at the change of seasons, which means the leaves are turning colors, the nights are getting cold, and the rain becomes a regular companion.


It’s a massive city with many changing neighborhoods. Our knowledgeable guide Martin was wise enough to rent us scooters for the ultimate tour of the city.

Martin in front of a section of the East Side Galleries

Having never been on a scooter before I was told it could be a bit tricky, and considering the fact that it was a new city loaded with people, cars, bikes, motorcycles, scooters, bikes, and bikes pulling wagons I was a bit nervous about navigating through without killing anyone. Myself included. But as we took off on our city tour, it became clear that this is without a doubt the best way to see a city. At every traffic light Martin would explain where we are and the sights that were all around. He’d even throw in a bit of history if the light was long enough. But zipping around these beautifully historic landscapes, over bridges, through roundabouts, from section to section I realized that not only is Berlin an amazing place, but my scooter and I were becoming the best of friends.

Martin showed us his version of Berlin which has many amazing people, excellent food, great bars and clubs, loads of empty spaces, incredible history, tons of pride, shit loads of bikes, and like the temperature this time of year, it is always changing.
