We obviously couldn’t leave Istanbul before doing one or three classic Turkish activities. So we saw the stunning Ayasofya which was burned down twice in its long history. It’s unique because it was originally a Basilica, later a Mosque and now a Museum...this is a nice big building, let’s use it for all sorts of religions. There is a beautiful restored virgin and child mosaic amongst others from the 9th century. Tons of snapping, flashing tourists attempt to dull their shine.

Time for the hamam. Time for a Turkish man with a moustache to scrub off a layer or two of skin. Time to lie on a marble baking pan and stare at the pin-pricked ceiling. Time to ponder the world. Time to get wrapped in towels and sent packing.

Time for hookah or ‘nargile’ as they call it. Not sure Cappucino was the best flavor choice. Revert to trusted apple. Ignite. Inhale. Enjoy.
